]]> Well, if you are looking for a Southern getaway in India, then the perfect choice of destination to plan your escape to is ‘God’s Own Country’ Kerala. A place which has a picturesque beauty located at the tropical Malabar Coast of India.

You can go as far as you can with your own imagination and find the best options to make your stay at Kerala come true with a lifetime of memories and adventures to look forward to.

Kochi is an ideal destination to spend winter in Kerala. Kochi’s magnificent Fort, the Chinese fishing nets, old Portuguese and Dutch-era houses, the crumbling remains of the British Era and the stunning shoreline makes this place so vintage and beautiful to visit. The best time you can visit Kochi is in December as the year-end carnival takes place during this time. You can enjoy bike races, fire displays, beach football, floor drawings and swimming competitions during this carnival which lasts for 10 days.

The beautiful hill station of Munnar features scenic views of the lush green tea estates, the misty valleys, and the slight chillness in the air which you can feel up amidst the mountains of this beautiful hill-station. There are several trekking routes here that you can enjoy and witness the breath-taking waterfalls cascading down the slopes. You can feel the old-world charm vibe of this hill station by staying at the beautiful looking Munnar Resorts.

Spreading across the Western Ghats, Wayanad is fondly known as the ‘The Land Of Paddy Fields’ as it holds a significant beauty amidst the lush green mountains. The dense forests, paddy fields, coconut palm plantations and the narrow winding roads will captivate your mind and soul. You can explore the Meenmutty Falls or Chembra Peak, visit the ancient architecture of Jain temples or trek to the Edakkal caves.

Witness nature in all its glory in Thekkady, this place which is at its full bloom in winter. Explore the Periyar National Park here which is a major attraction, go on jungle safaris and spot the Great Indian tiger, sambars, gaurs, leopards and more. You could attend the Garudan festival that takes place in the Kali temples or enjoy the wildlife boat rides along the Thekkady lake.

Mostly known as the ‘Venice of The East’, Alleppey is very famous for its backwaters. You live your dream of living in a houseboat surrounded by peaceful backwaters of Alleppey. The well-ornamented snake boats are the major attraction of this place. You can unravel the local culture and get a glimpse of the village life here, take an adventure on the water and experience the true heritage of Kerala.

Alappuzha (or Alleppey) is a city located here which is also known for houseboat cruises along the backwaters of Kerala. The city’s Mullakkal Temple features a traditional design. The most famous annual event here is Punnamada Lake’s snake boat races.

Idukki is also an enchanting hill station in Kerala. It is popularly known for its backwaters and is mostly visited during winters. Well, you explore the city and travelers can enjoy sightseeing tours, houseboat rides and even see the variety of flora and fauna in the region. Here the Idukki Dam was constructed along with two other dams at Cheruthoni and Kulamavu. The stored water here is used to produce electricity at the Moolamattom Powerhouse, which is located inside nearby rocky caves.

]]> It is often necessary to break the monotonous routine of mechanical world & taste some different flavors of life. Travelling is one of those different flavors which help us to discover the beauty of our life in a different way. Travelling not only helps us to meet with new people or to become familiar with different cultures but also to celebrate the beauty of our mother nature. Uttarakhand, which is in the northwestern part of India, is one of the most popular destinations for hodophiles of India & abroad.

Visitors love to travel to Uttarakhand for its artistry and heavenliness. It is known as “Devbhoomi” (Land of gods) as it is covered with many Hindu pilgrimage sites. Heavenly beauty of ‘Valley of Flowers” which is discovered in 1931, by British mountaineers who lost their way and came to this wonderful valley. They were mesmerized with the beauty of this place & named it the “Valley of Flowers”. It is the destination where nature blooms in full glory offering a heavenly experience.

Over 600 species of flowers are there in the valley of flowers e.g., orchids, poppies, primulas, marigold, daisies and anemones etc. It is situated at an altitude of around 3,600 m above the sea level. Rishikesh, which is also known as “The Land of God” is one of the most visiting sacred places in India where a good number of yogis come throughout the year. Visitors used to do several activities here such as Bungee, cliff jumping, Ver rafting etc. Beautiful places like Ram Jhula, Laxman Jhula, Rajaji National Park etc are the key attraction of Rishikesh.

The best time to visit Rishikesh is from March to the first week of May. Jim Corbett National Park is one of the most interesting destinations of Uttarakhand. It is named after Jim Corbett who is India’s best-known hunter & earned fame after he tracked down and killed several man-eating tigers and leopards. This park is also known as “The home of Royal Bengal Tiger”. The holy city of Haridwar, which is a sacred place of Hindu devotees, is another key attraction of Uttarakhand. Kumbh Mela in Haridwar which is held after each 12 years reflects the festivity of the river Ganga; one can indulge in many festive celebrations here.
