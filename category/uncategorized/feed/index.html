It is often necessary to break the monotonous routine of mechanical world & taste some different flavors of life. Travelling is one of those different flavors which help us to discover the beauty of our life in a different way. Travelling not only helps us to meet with new people or to become familiar with different cultures but also to celebrate the beauty of our mother nature. Uttarakhand, which is in the northwestern part of India, is one of the most popular destinations for hodophiles of India & abroad.

Visitors love to travel to Uttarakhand for its artistry and heavenliness. It is known as “Devbhoomi” (Land of gods) as it is covered with many Hindu pilgrimage sites. Heavenly beauty of ‘Valley of Flowers” which is discovered in 1931, by British mountaineers who lost their way and came to this wonderful valley. They were mesmerized with the beauty of this place & named it the “Valley of Flowers”. It is the destination where nature blooms in full glory offering a heavenly experience.

Over 600 species of flowers are there in the valley of flowers e.g., orchids, poppies, primulas, marigold, daisies and anemones etc. It is situated at an altitude of around 3,600 m above the sea level. Rishikesh, which is also known as “The Land of God” is one of the most visiting sacred places in India where a good number of yogis come throughout the year. Visitors used to do several activities here such as Bungee, cliff jumping, Ver rafting etc. Beautiful places like Ram Jhula, Laxman Jhula, Rajaji National Park etc are the key attraction of Rishikesh.

The best time to visit Rishikesh is from March to the first week of May. Jim Corbett National Park is one of the most interesting destinations of Uttarakhand. It is named after Jim Corbett who is India’s best-known hunter & earned fame after he tracked down and killed several man-eating tigers and leopards. This park is also known as “The home of Royal Bengal Tiger”. The holy city of Haridwar, which is a sacred place of Hindu devotees, is another key attraction of Uttarakhand. Kumbh Mela in Haridwar which is held after each 12 years reflects the festivity of the river Ganga; one can indulge in many festive celebrations here.

The sate of Tripura is tucked in mythology, has a history spanning more than 2,500 years and has had 186 King. The origin of the name Tripura remains a matter of debate between historians and researchers. According to ‘Rajmala “, the famous court history of Tripura, an ancient king called’ Tripur ‘ruled over the local domain known as’ Tripura’ and the name of the empire was derived from its name.

Tripura is the state of India, Hilly sate in northeast.  This is the 3rd smallest sate in India. Totally its covered 10,491 km. Surrounded on all sides by the deltaic basin of Bangladesh except for a small portion in the Northeast that meets the Cachar district of Assam and Mizoram.

Beauty of Tripura’s Culture:

Folk culture is the backbone of Tripura culture for tribal and non tribal people of Tripura. Tripura have their own traditional dress for their culture. Some of the experiences available in Tripurainclude the diversity of  culture streams and beliefs, Historic temples, handcrafts, traditional arts and music, dance, abundant biodiversity, and flushing meadows.

Siliguri, which has a population of 500,000, is one of the most significant cities in North Bengal.  Siliguri is both a commercial center and a stopover for travelers heading to various tourist spots in the northern part of the state. It is located along the river Teesta at the foothills of the Himalayas. Siliguri is the only city after Kolkata to have its airport, which makes it easier for the city to connect with the rest of the nation and to have excellent road connectivity with surrounding places. Here is a brief travel guide for visiting Siliguri and taking advantage of its tourism.

  1. Climate and the Ideal Time to Visit
  • From (April to June), the summer

Siliguri has exceptionally hot summers because it is not at a higher altitude but rather at the foothills of the Himalayas. The rising temperature is accompanied by a steady increase in humidity and heat. The majority of visitors the city encounters at this time are individuals who are using Siliguri as a stopover on route to hilly areas to avoid the heat.

  • (November to January) Winter:

Winter is the ideal season to go if you want to stay in the city and explore it. The temperature continues to fluctuate between 8°C and 25°C despite a significant drop. There are several magnificent tourist attractions in Siliguri that may be explored before continuing to Darjeeling to see the stunning Kanchenjungha, go paragliding in Kalimpong, go hiking in Sandakphu, or see wild animals in the Dooars. From Siliguri to Darjeeling, taking the toy train is highly advised.

  1. Things to Do
  • Go on a Safari:

Good news is available for wildlife enthusiasts. For the enjoyment of visitors, there is the Bengal Safari Park, where jeep and elephant safaris are planned. Elephants, tigers, leopards, and other wild animals are all housed in the park. As the jeeps transport the tourists very far into the forest, it is a thrill to see them lying around there. The atmosphere of calmness and serenity offers much-needed respite from the hustle and bustle of the metropolis.

  • Traveling by Himalayan Train:

One of the country’s last remaining mountain railways is the 200-year-old one in Darjeeling. It is appropriately recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It still runs often between Darjeeling and New Jalpaiguri and provides a stunning view of the surroundings, with mountains in the distance flowing through the vast tea estates. In the city, you absolutely must use the train.

  • Visiting Dudhia for a Day:

On the banks of the River Balaton, the hamlet of Dudhia is incredibly attractive. Only 25 kilometers from Siliguri, this breathtaking location is a popular tourist destination. It is a well-liked location for overnight camping or family picnics. It is a great camp experience out beneath a sky that is clear and blue and watching the stars.

  • Discovering North Bengal’s Jewels:

For tourists traveling from Kolkata and other locations to the state’s northern regions, Siliguri serves as a base and a layover. It is worthwhile to explore and take in North Bengal’s incredible nature. All of these locations may be found close to Siliguri. To go to these locations and roll in the lap of nature, all it takes is a short bus or car ride, or even a quick train ride. Siliguri serves as the entry point to some of West Bengal’s top tourist sites, including Darjeeling, the Dooars, Kalimpong, and Mirik.

  1. Eating in Siliguri
  • Siliguri experiences quite a mix of cultures which is manifested in their cuisine. Tourists get treated to the delicious Bengali cuisine with flavourful vegetarian and non-vegetarian dishes, and mouthwatering Chinese and Tibetan food as well. Siliguri has a sizeable Tibetan population which accounts for the amazing tulpas and momos that you will find here. For sweets lovers, numerous Bengali sweet shops are selling amazing sweets. When in Siliguri there is no dearth of good food.
  1. Shopping in Siliguri:
  • Because of the well-known Hong Kong Market, Siliguri is a shoppers’ paradise. An ancient market in Hakim Para that sells a wide variety of imported goods may be found here. International brands can be bought for incredibly low prices when buying clothing and electronics. To their heart’s content, people come here to shop. Particularly affordable are electronic products. If you’ve had a long day of shopping, treat yourself to some delicious Bengali and Chinese food at the nearby Haveli Restaurant.

Rajasthan, the ‘Land of Kings’, located in northwest India is one of the most visited and largest state in India that gives you the once-in-a-lifetime opportunity to experience the historical and cultural gems, with its legendary places, forts. The land of Rajasthan has been ruled by royals including the Rajputs, Mughals and Jats. They bought their own style and architecture that later blended into a mesmerizing sight that will blow your mind. 

A trip to Rajasthan can’t be done without visiting the Blue City of Jodhpur, marvel at the golden landscape Jaisalmer Fort, the 36-kilometre long fortification wall of the Kumbhalgarh Fort (the world’s second-longest wall), the City Palace of Jaipur and Udaipur, grand architecture of Chittorgarh Fort.

Rajasthan is also known for its palace-like hotels that offer a royal ambiance giving you royal feelings. The Umaid Bhawan Palace, Taj Lake Palace, Rambagh Palace, Samode Palace are some of the most well-knowned heritage hotels.

Royal life in not complete without the royal elephant rides. When it’s about royal rides Jaipur is the first place that comes into our mind. The pink city offers you the grandness of elephant rides.

Wildlife lovers would love to visit the Ranthambore National Park, Keoladeo Ghana Bird Sanctuary, Sariska Tiger Reserve, and Kumbhalgarh Wildlife Sanctuary, where you can get a glimpse of the Royal Bengal tigers, rare Great Indian bustard, river dolphins, and several animals.

Rajasthan is also famous for its colorful festivals and fairs. The Teej Festival honours the union of Lord Shiva and Goddess Parvati, the Rajasthan International Folk Festival, the Pushkar Fair, which is one of India’s largest camel, horse and cattle fairs. Also the Elephant Fair, the Desert Festival and International Kite Festival can give you reasons the visit Rajasthan for sure.

The colorful bazaars in Rajasthan have their charms. You can get a verity of things from gold and traditional jewelry to spices, trinkets, antiques, and handicrafts. Some best shopping spots you can visit are Johari Bazaar in Jaipur, the Clock Tower Market in Jodhpur, and the Bada Bazaar in Udaipur.

When you visit a place you can’t forget about the people. Rajasthan’s people are traditional and down-to-earth and the adage, ‘Atithi Devo Bhava‘ is in their culture.

A place filled with the essence of history, totally different from other visiting places, every brick of the wall here has some history to tell. This is a city which was invaded 18 times during its history to plunder the treasures of the temple , rather than for religious reasons. It is a city in the state of Odisha in eastern India. It is also called Sri Jagannath Dhama after the 12th-century Jagannath Temple located in the city. 

Puri is famous for the world famous Jagannath Temple & Longest Golden Beach. Out of four Dhamas It is one of the Dhamas (Holiest of the holy place) i.e. Puri, Dwarika, Badrinath & Rameswar, in India. Lord Jagannath, Devi Subhadra and elder brother Balabhadra are being worshipped in Puri (The Purusottama Kshetra)

In 1906, an exponent of Kriya Yoga and a resident of Puri, established an ashram, a spiritual training center, named “Kararashram” in Puri. He died on 9 March 1936 and his body is buried in the garden of the ashram.
For the people of Puri, Lord Jagannatha, visualized as Lord Krishna, is synonymous with their city. They believe that Lord Jagannatha looks after the welfare of the state.
One thing to note that, non-Hindus are not permitted to enter the shrines but are allowed to view the temple and the proceedings from the roof of the Raghunandan library, located within the precincts of the temple, for a small donation.
The climate of Puri is classified as Aw (Tropical savanna climate). The city has moderate and tropical climate. Humidity is fairly high throughout the year. The temperature during summer is very hot and during winter it is moderate temepereture, so winter would be the best time to visit this place.

Cultural activities, including the annual religious festivals, in Puri are: The Puri Beach Festival held from 5 to 9 November every year, and the Shreekshetra Utsav held from 20 December to 2 January every year. The cultural programmes include unique sand art, display of local and traditional handicrafts and food festival. In addition, cultural programmes are held for two hours on every second Saturday of the month at the district Collector’s Conference Hall near Sea Beach Police Station. Odissi dance, Odissi music and folk dances are part of this event. Odissi dance is the cultural heritage of Puri.

The Pancha Tirtha of Puri are (Five sacred bathing places of puri):
• Indradyumana Tank,
• Rohini Kunda
• Markandeya Tank
• Swetaganga Tank
• The Sea

And the Notable Temples of Puri are:

• Alamchandi Temple
• Bata Mangala Temple
• Chakra Narasimha Temple
• Lokanatha Temple
• Markandeshwar Temple
• Mausi Maa Temple
• Narasimha Temple, Puri
